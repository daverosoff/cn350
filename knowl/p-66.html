The inductive case is always phrased as a conditional. “If \(n\) is a natural number that is in \(S\text{,}\) then \(n+1\) is also in \(S\text{.}\)” If you recall the definition of \(S\text{,}\) you will see that this conditional is equivalent to
\begin{equation*} \left( P_n \implies Q_n \right) \implies \left( P_{n+1} \implies Q_{n+1} \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
So, we usually formulate our induction step in the latter way. My induction hypothesis would be “\(P_n\) implies \(Q_n\)”. From this hypothesis I would attempt to deduce the conclusion, “\(P_{n+1}\) implies \(Q_{n+1}\)”.

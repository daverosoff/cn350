Negate the following statement:
\(\text{If Mary fails her classes, then she cannot graduate.}\)
\(\text{p: Mary fails her classes}\)
\(\text{q: Mary can graduate}\)
Write the statement in formal logic:
  • \(\displaystyle p\rightarrow \mathbin{\sim} q\)
  • \(\displaystyle \mathbin{\sim} p\rightarrow q\)
  • \(\displaystyle p\rightarrow q\)
  • \(\displaystyle q\rightarrow p\)
Negate the logic:
  • \(\displaystyle \mathbin{\sim} p\wedge \mathbin{\sim} q\)
  • \(\displaystyle p\wedge q\)
  • \(\displaystyle \mathbin{\sim} p\wedge q\)
  • \(\displaystyle \mathbin{\sim} p\vee \mathbin{\sim} q\)
Rewrite the negated logic in English
  • Mary does not fail her classes and she can graduate
  • Mary does not fail her classes and she cannot graduate
  • Mary does not fail her classes or she cannot graduate
  • Mary fails her classes and she can graduate

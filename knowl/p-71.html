The inductive case is always phrased as a conditional. “If \(n\) is a natural number that is in \(A\text{,}\) then \(n+1\) is also in \(A\text{.}\)” Let us prove this statement. We will use a direct proof, assuming the hypothesis (\(3\) divides \(4^n - 1\)) and deducing the conclusion (\(3\) divides \(4^{n+1} - 1\)).

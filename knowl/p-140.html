This is not an induction proof. There is no way to divide this into a sequence of ordered “cases”. Instead, you will suppose (your hypothesis) that \(M\) has been given, and it accepts \(B\text{.}\) Use this assumption and what you have learned about DFA to argue that when the states are swapped, the resulting machine accepts all words not in \(B\) and rejects all words in \(B\text{.}\) Since you don’t have a transition diagram to fall back on, you’ll need to use the formal description of the DFA in terms of sets and functions.

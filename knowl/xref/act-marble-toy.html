Activity 2.1.1.

(This example is due to HMU.) Consider the marble-rolling toy pictured below. A marble enters at either \(A\) or \(B\text{.}\) The path taken by the marble is determined by three levers. Lever 1 is immediately beneath A. If it points left, the marble rolls left and exits through output C. If Lever 1 points right, the marble rolls right and lands on Lever 2 (between A and B). If Lever 2 points left, the marble rolls left and exits through output C. If Lever 2 points rights, the marble rolls right and exits through output D. Finally, Lever 3 is beneath input B. If it points left, we go to Lever 2; otherwise, to output D.
Let us say that a word is accepted if, when we begin in the pictured starting configuration, rolling a marble for each symbol, the final marble exits at \(D\text{.}\)
  1. Does the toy accept \(a\text{?}\)
  2. Does the toy accept \(b\text{?}\)
  3. Does the toy accept \(ab\text{?}\)
  4. Does the toy accept \(ba\text{?}\)
  5. Does the toy accept \(aab\text{?}\)
  6. Does the toy accept \(bab\text{?}\)
  7. Find all the words on \(\{ a,b \}\) of length at most \(4\) accepted by the marble-rolling toy, assuming that a word is accepted if the last marble exits at D.

    2. \(\displaystyle a\)

Activity 2.1.9.

Let \(A = (Q, \Sigma, \delta, q_0, \{q_f\})\) be a DFA, and suppose that for all \(a \in \Sigma\) we have
\begin{equation*} \delta(q_0, a) = \delta(q_f, a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
  1. Show that for all \(w \ne \varepsilon\) we have \(\hat{\delta}(q_0, w) = \hat{\delta}(q_f, w)\text{.}\)
  2. Show that if \(x\) is a nonempty string in \(L(A)\text{,}\) then for all \(k > 0\text{,}\) then \(x^k\text{,}\) the \(k\)-fold concatenation of \(x\text{,}\) is also in \(L(A)\text{.}\)

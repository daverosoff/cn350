Solution 1.

\(\text{Mike and Karen are tall.}\) Let \(p\) be the statement \(\text{Mike is tall}\) Let \(q\) be the statement \(\text{Karen is tall}\) First, translate the statement into formal logic. In this statement, \(p\) is true, and \(q\) is true. Therefore, the correct answer is \(p\) and \(q\text{,}\) or \(p\wedge q\text{.}\) Now, negate the statement. \(\mathbin{\sim} (p\wedge q)\) \(\equiv ( \mathbin{\sim} p\vee \mathbin{\sim} q)\) Thus, the answer is \(\mathbin{\sim} p\vee \mathbin{\sim} q\) Recall what \(p\) and \(q\) represent: \(\text{p: Mike is tall}\) \(\text{q: Karen is tall}\) From \(\mathbin{\sim} p\vee \mathbin{\sim} q\text{,}\) we get \(\text{Mike is not tall, or Karen is not tall.}\)
